# Fifty Strategic Steps: A Comprehensive Economic Growth Guide for Lebanon, Virginia

## A Detailed Action Plan for Transforming Southwest Virginia's County Seat into a Regional Economic Hub

---

## Section 1: Agricultural Expansion & Modernization

### Step 1: Establish the Large Animal Veterinary Incentive Program

Lebanon must immediately implement the $40,000 veterinary incentive program created through the partnership between the Governor's Agriculture and Forestry Industries Development (AFID) Fund and the Virginia Tobacco Region Revitalization Commission . This program will recruit large animal veterinarians to the region by offering financial and logistical support to new practitioners, thereby addressing the critical shortage that currently limits cattle herd expansion . Russell County's donation of 2.3 acres of land to house facilities and equipment leased to prospective veterinarians provides the physical infrastructure necessary for new practitioners to build a practice quickly and deliver services promptly . The recruitment of 2-3 large animal veterinarians within the first 24 months will enable cattle producers to expand their operations without being constrained by veterinary access, supporting an increase in cattle inventory from 37,000 to 44,000 within five years. This investment, supported by new state legislation (HB 2303 and SB 921) directing the State Veterinarian to establish a large animal veterinary grant program by July 2026, creates a sustainable pipeline for veterinary services that will underpin the region's agricultural growth for decades .

### Step 2: Expand the Virginia Quality Assured (VQA) Feeder Cattle Program

Russell County producers must increase participation in the VQA feeder cattle program, which has successfully marketed over 125,000 head since 1997, generating $6.4 million in value-added income. The program, which operates through the Abingdon Feeder Cattle Association, has demonstrated that certified calves command premium prices in the marketplace, with the partnership between Virginia Tech, the Virginia Cattlemen's Association, and the Tri-State Livestock Market providing the technical and marketing infrastructure for success. By expanding VQA certification to cover 25-30% of the county's annual calf crop, producers can capture an additional $1.5-2.0 million in premium income annually. The program's collaborative model, which includes information sharing on health, performance, and carcass merit, has been instrumental in establishing Russell County's reputation for high-quality feeder cattle. Continued growth of the VQA program requires sustained investment in producer education and marketing, with the Russell County Cattlemen's Association serving as the primary vehicle for outreach and coordination.

### Step 3: Adopt Precision Agriculture Technologies

Lebanon's cattle producers must embrace precision agriculture technologies, including TMR feeding robots, RFID and GPS livestock tracking systems, and drone-based pasture and herd monitoring, to improve efficiency and reduce labor costs. TMR feeding robots, which cost between $20,000 and $50,000, achieve payback periods of two to three years through feed efficiency improvements of 10-20% and labor savings of one to two full-time equivalent positions. Feed pushing robots, costing $10,000-$25,000, achieve similar payback periods while improving productivity by 5-10%. Health monitoring systems, costing $5,000-$15,000, achieve payback in one to two years through early disease detection and reduced veterinary costs. By targeting 100 farms for TMR robot adoption, 150 for feed pushers, and 200 for health monitoring systems, the county can capture $3.5-$8.5 million in annual savings and productivity improvements, creating a more resilient and profitable agricultural sector.

### Step 4: Develop a Regional Meat Processing Facility

Lebanon must develop a modern USDA-inspected meat processing facility to capture greater value from the region's cattle production. The Tazewell County Industrial Development Authority's meat processing facility project, which received a loan closing deadline extension to June 11, 2027, demonstrates regional interest in this critical infrastructure . A facility costing $5-10 million, supported by VCEDA loans and private investment, would provide USDA Choice and Select grading for military and premium markets, dry-aging facilities for premium beef, and vacuum-sealing and packaging for direct-to-consumer sales. The facility would capture 30-50% more value from cattle production, potentially generating $10-18 million annually in additional revenue for local producers. The integration of cold chain logistics, with refrigerated truck fleets featuring GPS tracking and IoT temperature monitoring, would support distribution to markets within a day's drive, including Washington D.C., Charlotte, Nashville, and Atlanta.

### Step 5: Establish Branded Beef Products

Lebanon must develop distinctive brands for its premium beef products, including "Russell County Premium Angus," "Appalachian Heritage Beef," and "Clinch Mountain Dry-Aged." The concept of place-based branding, which associates products with the geographic origin and cultural heritage of the region, can create powerful consumer connections that support premium pricing. The Crooked Road Heritage Music Trail provides a rich narrative context for branding, with the potential to appeal to consumers interested in authentic regional experiences. Branded beef products, targeting the upscale dining and retail markets in major metropolitan areas within a day's drive, can command premiums of 30-100% above commodity beef prices. The integration of branding with agritourism and direct-to-consumer sales channels would create synergies that support both agricultural and tourism development, generating additional economic activity in the region.

### Step 6: Expand the Regional Dairy Cooperative

Lebanon should support the expansion of dairy production through the development of a regional dairy cooperative that pools production from multiple farms to improve efficiency and marketing. The expansion of Bates Family Farm, which is converting its former manufacturing building into a creamery to produce bottled goat milk and cheeses, demonstrates the potential for value-added dairy processing . The cooperative model enables small dairy operations to achieve economies of scale, improving access to processing, marketing, and distribution infrastructure. The development of artisan cheese, butter, yogurt, and ice cream products, leveraging the region's cooler climate and high-quality milk, would create additional revenue streams for dairy producers. The integration of dairy processing with agritourism, including farm tours and tasting rooms, would create additional economic activity and support the region's tourism development.

### Step 7: Develop Hay and Forage Production

Lebanon must improve hay and forage production to support the region's cattle industry and reduce reliance on purchased feed. The 26,305 acres of forage-land used for hay, haylage, grass silage, and greenchop in Russell County provide the foundation for expansion, with the potential to increase production through improved varieties, rotational grazing, and soil fertility management. The adoption of soil testing, lime application, and fertilization based on soil needs can increase forage yields by 20% or more, supporting a corresponding increase in cattle carrying capacity. The integration of cover crops and improved pasture management can improve soil health and extend the grazing season, reducing winter feed costs. By increasing forage production by 15-20%, producers can reduce feed costs by $500-$1,000 per cow, improving profitability and supporting herd expansion.

### Step 8: Utilize Climate-Smart Agriculture Programs

Lebanon's farmers must participate in the Grazing for Appalachian Sustainability (GRASS) project, which has over $3,500,000 available to incentivize sustainable grazing practices . The project, a partnership led by West Virginia University Extension and funded through the USDA Partnerships for Climate-Smart Commodities, provides annual cash incentives of approximately $20,000 per producer for implementation of conservation practices. Approved practices include alley cropping, silvopasture, fencing, pasture and hay planting, prescribed grazing, nutrient management, and tree and shrub establishment. Participating farmers may also qualify for $5,000 per producer for reporting assistance and additional compensation for participation in grazing schools and mentoring programs. By enrolling 50-100 farmers in the program over three years, Lebanon can capture $1-2 million in federal funding while adopting sustainable practices that improve soil health and productivity.

### Step 9: Establish a County Extension Robotics Training Program

Lebanon must partner with Virginia Tech's Cooperative Extension Service to establish a robotics training program specifically focused on agricultural automation. The program would train farmers and farm workers in the operation and maintenance of TMR feeding robots, feed pushing robots, health monitoring systems, and drone-based pasture monitoring. The training would be offered through the Russell County Career and Technical Center, with support from Southwest Virginia Community College's recently awarded $999,868 USDA grant for distance learning equipment. The program would include both classroom instruction and hands-on training, with participants receiving certifications that demonstrate their competence in operating and maintaining agricultural robotics. The availability of trained personnel would accelerate automation adoption and create a skilled workforce that supports the region's technology sector.

### Step 10: Develop a Farmer-to-Farmer Mentorship Program

Lebanon must leverage the region's experienced farmers, including the operators of Century Farms such as Stuart Land and Cattle Company, to provide mentorship to beginning farmers. The program would pair experienced producers with new farmers to share knowledge about cattle production, pasture management, and value-added processing. The mentorship model, which has been successfully implemented by the Virginia Cattlemen's Association, accelerates the learning curve for new producers and improves retention rates. The program would also provide a pipeline for the transfer of farmland to the next generation, addressing the demographic challenges facing the region's agricultural sector. The successful model of Smithfield Farms, which has operated continuously since 1774, demonstrates the value of intergenerational knowledge transfer in sustaining agricultural operations.

## Section 2: Workforce Development & Education

### Step 11: Launch the Hospitality and Tourism Management Pilot Program

Lebanon must support the new Hospitality and Tourism Management Trainee and Culinary, Restaurant, and Event Management Pilot Program at the University of Virginia's College at Wise, funded by a $230,016 grant from the VCEDA Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund . This program will provide workforce training for the region's growing tourism and hospitality sectors, creating a pipeline of skilled workers for hotels, restaurants, event venues, and golf operations. The program will offer hands-on training in event management, culinary arts, and hospitality operations, with participants gaining practical experience through internships with local businesses. The success of this pilot program, which is the first of its kind in the region, could lead to expansion and replication across Southwest Virginia, creating a skilled workforce that supports the region's tourism development.

### Step 12: Establish the Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund

Lebanon must maximize the use of the VCEDA Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund, which provides loans and grants for curriculum development, training equipment, training facilities construction, instructional materials, stipends, scholarships, tuition, internships, and training or retraining instructional costs . The fund's Coalfield Jobs Investment Project (CJIP) provides grants of $2,500 per new qualifying full-time job created in the region within targeted industry sectors . By leveraging these resources, Lebanon can support workforce development in sectors including information technology, manufacturing, energy, creative tourism, and agricultural technology. The fund's flexible approach, which allows for a range of training programs and activities, enables Lebanon to tailor workforce development to the specific needs of its employers and industries.

### Step 13: Develop Modular Housing Production and Construction Training

Lebanon must support the Appalachian Highlands Housing Partners (AHHP) initiative, which is developing a modular housing production facility that also focuses on construction training and education . The facility, supported by a $3 million VCEDA loan to the Russell County Industrial Development Authority, will manufacture modular housing units while providing training in modern construction techniques, including electrical, plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, engineering, and project management . The goal is to create a pipeline of 89 new jobs within five years while also addressing the workforce housing shortage that has constrained regional growth . The training program will equip workers with in-demand skills in modern construction, creating lasting economic impact while providing affordable housing for hardworking families earning 80-120% of the Area Median Income . By providing both employment and housing, this initiative creates a virtuous cycle that supports population growth and economic development.

### Step 14: Establish the Dickenson County Community Business Launch Program

Lebanon should draw lessons from the Dickenson County Community Business Launch program and small business incubator space at the Dickenson Center for Education and Research, which received a $42,500 VCEDA grant . The program provides entrepreneurship training and support to individuals seeking to start businesses in the region, addressing the gap between business ideas and successful launch. The incubator space provides affordable office and retail space for startups, with mentoring and technical assistance available through the program. The model, which focuses on building local business capacity, supports the diversification of the region's economy by enabling residents to turn their business ideas into viable enterprises. The program's success has been demonstrated through the creation of multiple new businesses, with participants achieving higher survival rates than the national average for startups.

### Step 15: Expand the VCEDA Seed Capital Matching Fund

Lebanon must promote the VCEDA Seed Capital Matching Fund, which provides matching grants of up to $10,000 to assist new, startup, and emerging small businesses . The fund, which has assisted more than 200 new business startups since its establishment in 2017, provides essential seed capital for businesses in qualifying categories. Recent recipients in the Russell County area include River Hill Farms ($10,000), PBall's Smoke & Grill LLC ($10,000), and several other startups . The fund requires a dollar-for-dollar match, encouraging entrepreneurs to invest their own resources while providing critical funding for startup costs. The program's success has been recognized by a 2021 Virginia Tech study, which found a return on investment ratio of 34:1, demonstrating the substantial economic impact of this program. By expanding awareness of the program and providing support for applicants, Lebanon can increase the number of successful startups in the community.

### Step 16: Establish the Small Developer Workshop for Workforce Housing

Lebanon must support the Small Developer Workshop program, which received a $29,500 grant from VCEDA to assist with regional capacity-building training in workforce housing . The program provides training for public officials, public employees, and stakeholders in the VCEDA region on workforce housing development, addressing the shortage of affordable housing that constrains economic growth. The workshop covers topics including financing, site selection, construction techniques, and regulatory compliance, enabling participants to develop housing projects that meet the needs of the region's workforce. The program's success in Dickenson County, which has seen the development of multiple housing projects as a result of the training, demonstrates the potential for replication across the region. By investing in housing development capacity, Lebanon can ensure that its workforce has access to affordable housing, supporting population growth and economic expansion.

### Step 17: Partner with Southwest Virginia Community College for Customized Workforce Training

Lebanon must leverage the $250,000 grant to the Southwest Virginia Community College Educational Foundation from the VCEDA Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund, which provides scholarships for the emerging workforce, training instructional costs, and customized workforce training for area businesses . The grant supports work-based learning opportunities, assessments, and National Career Readiness Certificate testing for residents and workers in the VCEDA region . By partnering with the college on customized training programs, Lebanon can address the specific workforce needs of its employers, including agricultural technology, manufacturing, healthcare, and hospitality. The college's recently awarded $999,868 USDA grant for distance learning equipment will enhance its capacity to deliver training to students across the region, supporting the development of the skilled workforce needed for emerging industries.

## Section 3: Tourism & Recreation Development

### Step 18: Complete Hillside Trail Park as a Tourism Gateway

Lebanon must complete the $50,000 VCEDA-funded Hillside Trail Park on the 11-acre site described by the Russell County Industrial Development Authority as the "gateway for broader tourism efforts tied to the Clinch River" . The park, which is being developed with matching funds from the Virginia Department of Forestry, the Town of Lebanon, and the Clinch Valley Soil and Water Conservation District, will emphasize low-impact development and provide opportunities for environmental education and community engagement . The park's goals include preserving and enhancing existing natural areas, improving positive environmental functions and water quality, providing accessible opportunities for environmental education, ensuring ADA-compliant access, increasing native habitat and biodiversity, and offering passive recreation options . The park lies within the Clinch River Watershed, with water from the site flowing through Big Cedar Creek into the Clinch River State Park, Virginia's first blueway state park, creating a connection to the region's broader conservation and tourism assets.

### Step 19: Expand River Hill Farms Fly Fishing Destination

Lebanon must support River Hill Farms, a $10,000 VCEDA Seed Capital Grant recipient that is developing a premier fly fishing destination along Big Cedar Creek . The 65-acre property, which fronts a one-mile stretch of pristine waters teeming with wild rainbow and brown trout, is developing a fishing lodge and guide service that projects one full-time and three part-time employees within five years . The lodge, which is 75-80% complete with a projected opening in early spring 2026, will be available for rental to those seeking a mountain getaway, with fly fishing packages available as an add-on . Future plans include adding yurts and cottages in a phased approach, creating a comprehensive outdoor tourism destination. The success of River Hill Farms demonstrates the potential for nature-based tourism to create jobs and economic activity while preserving the region's natural resources.

### Step 20: Develop Cedar Fest as a Signature Event

Lebanon must continue to develop Cedar Fest, which has received a $5,000 grant from the Virginia Tourism Corporation's Special Events and Festivals Sponsorship Program, requiring $5,000 in matching funds . The two-day festival continues to grow in popularity, bringing visitors from all over the region and outside the region to see a mix of great musical entertainment in a small-town atmosphere . With the backdrop of the Clinch Mountain range on one side and Beartown Mountain directly behind the stage, the festival offers a picturesque setting that has been recognized as unparalleled in the region . Cedar Fest won the Southwest Virginia Tourism award for Outstanding Festival of the Year in the under 10,000 attendees category, demonstrating the quality of the event and its potential for continued growth . The Virginia Tourism Corporation's hub-and-spoke tourism model, which encourages visitors to extend their stays to experience local attractions, can be leveraged to increase the festival's economic impact . By expanding the festival's programming and marketing, Lebanon can attract additional visitors and create year-round tourism activity.

### Step 21: Integrate Lebanon into the Crooked Road Heritage Music Trail

Lebanon must strengthen its role along The Crooked Road: Virginia's Heritage Music Trail, the 333-mile driving trail linking over 60 music venues across Southwest Virginia. The Crooked Road's affiliated venues and festivals program, which includes three classifications of venues and events, provides a framework for formalizing Lebanon's cultural offerings. Cedar Fest's affiliation with The Crooked Road provides promotional support, including listing on the Crooked Road website, promotion through Crooked Road media, and the opportunity to display the Affiliate banner. The development of a performing arts center, similar to the Jettie Baker Center in Clintwood, could provide a year-round venue for music performances, theater, and community events. The integration of Lebanon with the Crooked Road network would create connections to the broader cultural tourism ecosystem, attracting visitors who come to experience the region's musical heritage.

### Step 22: Develop a Championship Golf Course as an Anchor Attraction

Lebanon must develop a championship golf course that leverages the region's natural beauty and strategic location to attract visitors from across the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast. The course should be designed to integrate with the existing topography, creating a challenging and visually stunning experience that showcases the Clinch Mountain region. The course should occupy 200-300 acres, with a mix of par 3, par 4, and par 5 holes, greens constructed to USGA specifications, and bunkers designed for visual interest and strategic challenge. Turfgrass selection should focus on cool-season grasses, including bentgrass for greens and tall fescue for fairways, adapted to the region's climate of hot, humid summers and cold, wet winters. The course should include practice facilities, a clubhouse with dining and event space, and GPS-equipped carts. The integration of the golf course with the region's cultural tourism assets creates a powerful draw for extended-stay visitors, supporting hotels, restaurants, and other businesses.

### Step 23: Establish Agritourism Experiences

Lebanon must develop agritourism experiences that connect visitors with the region's agricultural heritage and production. Farm-to-table dining experiences, which feature locally produced beef, dairy, and produce, would attract culinary tourists and support local farmers. Cattle ranch tours, which allow visitors to see cattle operations and learn about production practices, would provide educational experiences that build appreciation for agriculture. Artisan food products, including cheese, maple syrup, and value-added beef products, would provide retail opportunities that generate additional income for producers. Agritourism events, including harvest festivals and cattle sales, would attract visitors and create year-round economic activity. The integration of agritourism with golf and cultural tourism would create a comprehensive visitor experience that encourages longer stays and increased spending.

### Step 24: Pursue Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Funds

Lebanon must seek VCEDA Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Fund grants for tourism-related capital improvements that are part of larger regional tourism initiatives . The fund has supported projects including the $50,000 grant for HVAC improvements at the Jettie Baker Center in Dickenson County and the $50,000 grant for the Hillside Trail Park in Lebanon . For Lebanon, potential projects include development of a performing arts center, expansion of outdoor recreation facilities, and improvements to festival infrastructure. The fund requires matching funds, encouraging local investment in tourism development. By strategically pursuing VCEDA funding, Lebanon can develop the tourism infrastructure needed to attract visitors and support economic growth in the hospitality sector.

### Step 25: Develop a Comprehensive Tourism Marketing Campaign

Lebanon must develop a comprehensive tourism marketing campaign that promotes the town's unique combination of golf, music, outdoor recreation, and agricultural experiences. The campaign should leverage the Virginia is for Lovers brand through the Virginia Tourism Corporation's programs . The campaign should target primary markets within a day's drive, including Washington D.C., Charlotte, Nashville, and Atlanta, with messaging that highlights the region's natural beauty and cultural authenticity. The campaign should use digital marketing, print advertising, public relations, and partnerships with travel organizations. The success of Cedar Fest in attracting visitors and winning awards demonstrates the effectiveness of marketing the region's unique cultural offerings. By developing a comprehensive marketing strategy, Lebanon can increase awareness of its tourism assets and attract visitors who will contribute to the local economy.

## Section 4: Manufacturing & Industrial Growth

### Step 26: Launch the Modular Housing Manufacturing Facility

Lebanon must support the Appalachian Highlands Housing Partners (AHHP) modular housing production facility, which received a $3 million VCEDA loan . The facility, located in the 92,000-square-foot former Buster Brown Building in the Russell County Industrial Park, will manufacture at least 260 modular housing units in the first year, with the capacity to generate 300-400 additional housing units annually as the workforce is trained and grows . The project anticipates the creation of 89 jobs within five years, with the housing units marketed throughout the VCEDA region . The facility also includes a workforce development component, training workers in modern construction techniques including electrical, plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, engineering, and project management . This initiative creates a virtuous cycle by providing both employment opportunities and affordable housing, supporting population growth and economic expansion.

### Step 27: Support Simmons Equipment Expansion

Lebanon must continue to support the expansion of Simmons Equipment, a mining equipment manufacturer that is expanding operations from Tazewell County into Russell County with an $8.5 million investment . VCEDA supported this expansion with a $500,000 loan to finance building upfits and improvements for the IDA's Russell Place property in Lebanon . The expansion creates jobs and economic activity in the region, while leveraging the region's manufacturing workforce and infrastructure. Simmons Equipment's presence in Russell County demonstrates the community's attractiveness to industrial employers and the effectiveness of public-private partnerships in supporting business growth. By continuing to support existing employers and facilitating their expansion, Lebanon can retain jobs and attract additional manufacturing investment to the region.

### Step 28: Pursue Updated Industrial Sites Study

Lebanon must leverage the updated industrial sites study recently commissioned by VCEDA, which will identify sites throughout the region suitable for future business and industry development . The study, being conducted by the Timmons Group, will provide detailed information on available industrial sites, including size, utilities, transportation access, and zoning . The study will serve as a critical tool for marketing the region to potential industrial investors and for prioritizing infrastructure investments. By identifying and preparing industrial sites for development, Lebanon can attract new manufacturing operations and support the expansion of existing businesses. The availability of prepared industrial sites is essential for capturing the growing interest in domestic manufacturing reshoring and attracting investment from companies seeking to diversify their supply chains.

### Step 29: Support Tate Data Center Components Expansion

Lebanon must support the expansion of Tate's data center components manufacturing operation, which has expanded from its St. Paul location to the former Alcoa building at 705 Regional Park Road in Russell County . The expansion, supported by a $4 million VCEDA loan, is projected to create 35 new full-time jobs within three years . The data center components sector is growing rapidly, with the new Tate operation representing a significant opportunity for Russell County to capture value from the expanding data center industry. The location of Tate's operations in the region provides opportunities for supply chain development, with potential for additional businesses to locate in the area to support the data center components industry. The success of Tate in the region demonstrates the potential for technology manufacturing to create jobs and economic activity in Russell County.

### Step 30: Develop Agricultural Robotics Manufacturing

Lebanon must develop agricultural robotics manufacturing capabilities, leveraging the LOWL compiler framework and the region's agricultural expertise. The assembly of TMR feeding robots, feed pushing robots, and health monitoring systems would create employment for engineers, technicians, assemblers, and support staff, while providing opportunities for local producers to access automation technology at lower cost. The investment range for an agricultural robotics assembly facility, estimated at $1 to $3 million, is modest compared to other manufacturing investments. The market for agricultural robotics extends beyond Russell County to include producers across Southwest Virginia, eastern Kentucky, northeastern Tennessee, and western North Carolina. The proximity to customers would provide advantages in terms of service and support, enabling Lebanon-based manufacturers to offer training, maintenance, and repair services that differentiate their products from those of distant competitors.

### Step 31: Expand White Rock Truss Operations

Lebanon must support the expansion of White Rock Truss & Components LLC, which recently celebrated a ribbon cutting in Lee County attended by the Governor . The expansion, supported by VCEDA loans, creates jobs and economic activity in the region while leveraging the region's workforce and infrastructure. White Rock Truss's success in the region demonstrates the potential for building components manufacturing to thrive in Southwest Virginia, taking advantage of the region's proximity to growing construction markets. By attracting additional building components manufacturers, Lebanon can create a cluster of businesses that support the region's construction and housing industries, creating jobs and economic activity that supports population growth.

### Step 32: Attract Additional Manufacturing Through VCEDA Financing

Lebanon must actively use VCEDA's financing programs to attract additional manufacturing operations to the region. The VCEDA Coalfield Revolving Loan Fund provides low-interest loans for fixed asset needs such as building construction, expansion, or rehabilitation, and the purchase of equipment, machinery, and tools . The VCEDA Communities for Opportunity Loan Fund provides financing for projects that create a unique opportunity in a specific community . These programs, combined with the VCEDA Workforce Development and Training Fund, provide a comprehensive financing package that can support manufacturing expansion. By promoting these programs to potential investors and working with VCEDA to facilitate financing, Lebanon can attract additional manufacturing operations that create jobs and economic activity in the region.

Lebanon should support the development of the Coalfields Expressway (CFX), which has received $3 million in federal funding from the Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2026 . The Virginia CFX Authority is preparing to apply for two federal U.S. Department of Transportation Better Utilizing Investments to Leverage Development (BUILD) grants . The CFX will improve transportation access to the region, reducing travel times and supporting economic development. The expressway will connect Lebanon and other communities to major transportation corridors, improving access to markets and supporting the growth of manufacturing, tourism, and other industries. By supporting the CFX development, Lebanon can improve its transportation infrastructure and attract additional investment to the region.

## Section 7: Strategic Planning & Implementation

### Step 44: Establish an Economic Development Dashboard

Lebanon must establish an economic development dashboard to track key metrics and measure progress toward economic development goals. The dashboard should track metrics including population growth, job creation, business formation, agricultural productivity, and tourism visitation. The dashboard should be updated regularly and shared with the public, providing transparency and accountability for economic development efforts. By tracking progress, Lebanon can identify areas of success and areas needing improvement, adjusting strategies as needed to achieve economic development goals. The dashboard would also serve as a communication tool, demonstrating progress to residents, businesses, and potential investors.

### Step 45: Develop a Long-Term Economic Development Strategy

Lebanon must develop a long-term economic development strategy that builds on the initiatives outlined in this guide. The strategy should include specific goals, objectives, and action steps, with timelines and responsible parties identified. The strategy should be developed through a collaborative process that includes input from residents, businesses, and community organizations. The strategy should be updated regularly to reflect changing conditions and emerging opportunities. By developing a comprehensive strategy, Lebanon can ensure that economic development efforts are coordinated and effective, avoiding duplication and maximizing impact.

### Step 46: Foster Public-Private Partnerships

Lebanon must foster public-private partnerships to support economic development, leveraging the resources of both sectors. The VCEDA financing programs, which provide loans and grants to support economic development, are a prime example of public-private partnerships that create jobs and economic activity . The Russell County IDA's work with private companies, including Bates Family Farm, Simmons Equipment, and AHHP, demonstrates the effectiveness of public-private partnerships in supporting business growth . By fostering additional public-private partnerships, Lebanon can leverage public resources to attract private investment, creating jobs and economic activity that benefits the community.

### Step 47: Develop a Tourism Marketing Strategy

Lebanon must develop a comprehensive tourism marketing strategy that promotes the town's unique combination of golf, music, outdoor recreation, and agricultural experiences. The strategy should leverage the Virginia Tourism Corporation's programs, including the Special Events and Festivals Sponsorship Program and the hub-and-spoke tourism model . The strategy should target primary markets within a day's drive, with messaging that highlights the region's natural beauty and cultural authenticity. The strategy should use digital marketing, print advertising, public relations, and partnerships with travel organizations. By developing a comprehensive marketing strategy, Lebanon can increase awareness of its tourism assets and attract visitors who will contribute to the local economy.

### Step 48: Engage the Community in Economic Development

Lebanon must engage the community in economic development, ensuring that residents have a voice in shaping the region's economic future. Community engagement should include public meetings, surveys, and advisory committees that provide input on economic development priorities. By engaging the community, Lebanon can build support for economic development initiatives and ensure that they reflect community values and priorities. The success of Cedar Fest, which has grown through community support and volunteer effort, demonstrates the power of community engagement in supporting economic development . By continuing to engage the community, Lebanon can build a foundation of support that sustains economic development efforts over the long term.

### Step 49: Pursue Regional Cooperation

Lebanon must pursue regional cooperation with neighboring communities and counties to support economic development. The VCEDA region, which includes Buchanan, Dickenson, Lee, Russell, Scott, Tazewell, and Wise counties and the City of Norton, provides a framework for regional cooperation . The Lonesome Pine Regional Industrial Facilities Authority provides a platform for regional industrial development, including the Intersection Business Park in Norton . The new initiative to generate leads for new businesses for the Intersection Business Park, conducted in coordination with the authority, demonstrates the potential for regional cooperation in economic development . By pursuing regional cooperation, Lebanon can access resources and opportunities that would not be available individually, supporting the growth of the broader region.

### Step 50: Establish a Continuous Improvement Process

Lebanon must establish a continuous improvement process that evaluates economic development efforts and identifies opportunities for improvement. The process should include regular review of economic development metrics, assessment of program effectiveness, and identification of emerging opportunities and challenges. The process should be informed by input from residents, businesses, and community organizations. By establishing a continuous improvement process, Lebanon can ensure that its economic development efforts remain effective and responsive to changing conditions. The success of the VCEDA Seed Capital Grant program, which has been continuously improved since its establishment in 2017, demonstrates the value of continuous improvement in economic development . By embracing continuous improvement, Lebanon can build on its successes and address challenges, creating a dynamic and resilient economy that serves the community for generations to come.

## Section 8: Leveraging Technology and the e-Region Model

### Step 51: Capitalize on Lebanon's Legacy as a Technology Pioneer

Lebanon must leverage its established reputation as a technology hub, building on the visionary work that began in the 1990s when regional leaders saw an opportunity to replace coal mining jobs with high-tech employment . The year 2006 marked a turning point when CGI, a Canadian company with a strong presence in Northern Virginia, located its first U.S. onshore delivery center in Lebanon . Within its first decade, CGI's Southwest Virginia Information Technology Center of Excellence had created more than 400 jobs and generated $68.5 million in annual economic impact . The same year, Northrop Grumman announced the opening of its Southwest Enterprise Solutions Center, also in Lebanon . This legacy of technology success provides a foundation for Lebanon to attract additional tech investment, as the region has already demonstrated its ability to support large-scale technology operations. The coalition responsible for making CGI's Lebanon facility a reality serves as a model for what communities can do when they share a commitment to innovation and economic growth .

### Step 52: Expand the e-Region Brand and Marketing Efforts

Lebanon must strengthen the e-Region brand, which positions Southwest Virginia as one of the East Coast's new centers for electronic information technology, energy, education, and emerging specialty manufacturing . VCEDA has branded this region as the e-Region, strategically located at the geographical center of commerce in the eastern U.S. . The e-Region's focus on technology has had ripple effects throughout the Southwest Virginia economy, with notable employers including SYKES Enterprises, which employs 750 in Wise concentrating on back-office operations . Work is currently underway to update marketing collateral including VCEDA's regional image booklet, sites and buildings brochures, postcards and other materials . By actively promoting the e-Region brand and participating in marketing events, Lebanon can attract new businesses and investment to the region. VCEDA has already participated in 22 marketing events and fielded 115 inquiries year-to-date, demonstrating the effectiveness of ongoing marketing efforts .

### Step 53: Leverage the UVa-Wise Software Engineering Pipeline

Lebanon must strengthen its partnership with the University of Virginia's College at Wise, which has the only undergraduate software engineering program in the Commonwealth . The university's comprehensive curriculum offers bachelor's degrees in computer science, math, software engineering, and management information systems . The six-month fast-track program developed to provide a ready workforce for CGI led the way, and today strategic partnerships continue to produce graduates who are ready for technology employment . The success of SYKES Enterprises in Wise is due in large part to the graduates of UVa-Wise . By promoting UVa-Wise's programs to potential employers and supporting the continued development of the university's technology curriculum, Lebanon can ensure a pipeline of skilled technology workers that attracts additional tech investment to the region.

### Step 54: Pursue Data Center Development Opportunities

Lebanon must capitalize on the growing data center industry in the region, which has demonstrated significant potential for job creation and economic impact. In 2017, DP Facilities opened its 65,000-square-foot Mineral Gap data center in Wise County, referred to by the company as "The Safest Place on Earth," a $65 million project employing 40 people . Future development of the campus will accommodate more than 200,000 square feet of data center space and administrative support buildings . Lebanon's advanced broadband infrastructure, strategic location, and availability of industrial sites make it an attractive location for data center operations. The updated industrial sites study recently commissioned by VCEDA, which will identify sites throughout the region suitable for future development for business and industry, should be leveraged to identify data center opportunities in Lebanon and Russell County .

### Step 55: Develop Emerging Manufacturing and Specialty Production

Lebanon must pursue emerging manufacturing opportunities, including specialty manufacturing that leverages the region's workforce and infrastructure. VCEDA has noted that manufacturing, distribution and logistics, information technology and seed capital have all been active sectors . Recent expansions by existing manufacturers in the region include White Rock Truss & Components LLC in Lee County and West River Conveyors in Buchanan County . The expansion of Tate's data center components manufacturing operation in Russell County, supported by a $4 million VCEDA loan, demonstrates the potential for specialty manufacturing to create jobs and economic activity . By continuing to identify and pursue emerging manufacturing opportunities, Lebanon can diversify its industrial base and create employment for the growing workforce.

## Section 9: Energy, Infrastructure, and Natural Resources

### Step 56: Support Natural Gas Pipeline Expansion

Lebanon must support the expansion of natural gas infrastructure, which is essential for industrial development and economic growth in the region. The Virginia state budget has provided $100,000 from the general fund to Russell County to explore expansion of a natural gas pipeline from the connection point of the current natural gas pipeline near Castlewood, Virginia, west along US Highway 58 to the Russell and Wise County line . This study will determine if utilizing available natural gas resources benefits economic development of the County . A similar $100,000 has been provided to Tazewell County to explore expansion of a natural gas pipeline to the Wardell Industrial Park . The VCEDA has also approved $1 million in the form of a $500,000 loan and a $500,000 grant to the Tazewell County IDA for natural gas utility infrastructure and improvements in the Richlands area . By supporting these pipeline expansion efforts, Lebanon can ensure that its industrial parks have access to affordable natural gas, attracting manufacturing and distribution operations to the region.

### Step 57: Explore Mine-Pool Geothermal Energy

Lebanon must explore the potential of mine-pool geothermal energy, which could provide sustainable heating and cooling for industrial and commercial facilities. The VCEDA has approved a $50,000 grant to the Buchanan County IDA to help finance a mine-pool geothermal feasibility study for the Southern Gap area in Buchanan County . The study will assess the potential for using mine-pool geothermal energy for heating and cooling applications, potentially providing sustainable energy for industrial and commercial facilities. The success of this study could lead to the development of geothermal energy projects that reduce energy costs and support sustainable economic development. For Lebanon, the lessons learned from this study could be applied to potential geothermal projects in Russell County, leveraging the region's mining heritage for sustainable energy production .

### Step 58: Expand Broadband and Telehealth Services

Lebanon must continue to expand broadband access and telehealth services to improve healthcare access and support economic development. The VCEDA has approved a $125,000 grant for Buchanan County to provide unserved and/or underserved citizens with telehealth and telemedicine needs using low earth orbit space-based broadband . This grant has been extended to include first responders and emergency services agencies serving Buchanan County . The VCEDA has also approved a $250,000 grant to the Southwest Virginia Community College Educational Foundation for workforce development and training, which includes support for National Career Readiness Certificate testing and other assessments . By expanding broadband and telehealth services, Lebanon can improve quality of life and healthcare access, supporting population growth and workforce development.

## Section 10: Arts, Culture, and Tourism

### Step 59: Complete the Hospitality and Tourism Management Pilot Program

Lebanon must fully implement the Hospitality and Tourism Management Trainee and Culinary, Restaurant, and Event Management Trainee Pilot Program at UVa-Wise, funded by a $230,016 grant from the VCEDA Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund . This program will provide workforce training for the region's growing tourism and hospitality sectors, creating a pipeline of skilled workers for hotels, restaurants, event venues, and tourism operations. The program will offer hands-on training in event management, culinary arts, and hospitality operations, with participants gaining practical experience through internships with local businesses. By fully implementing this program and promoting its graduates to local employers, Lebanon can build a skilled workforce that supports the region's tourism development and attracts additional investment in the hospitality sector.

### Step 60: Support Jettie Baker Center Improvements and Arts Venues

Lebanon must support the development of arts venues, drawing lessons from the Jettie Baker Center in Clintwood, which received a $50,000 grant from the VCEDA Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Fund for HVAC improvements . The Jettie Baker Center serves as a performing arts venue for the region, hosting music performances, theater, and community events. By supporting the development of similar venues in Lebanon, including the recently completed Russell Theater, the town can create year-round arts programming that attracts visitors and supports the local economy. The integration of arts venues with The Crooked Road Heritage Music Trail and Cedar Fest would create a comprehensive cultural tourism experience that encourages extended stays and increased spending.

### Step 61: Develop Seed Capital Grant Recipients into Successful Businesses

Lebanon must support the businesses that have received VCEDA Seed Capital Grants, ensuring they become successful and sustainable operations. Recent Russell County recipients include River Hill Farms ($10,000), a premier fly fishing destination along Big Cedar Creek, and PBall's Smoke & Grill LLC ($10,000) . These businesses, along with others in the region, have received seed capital to support their startup operations. By providing mentoring, technical assistance, and connections to additional financing, Lebanon can help these businesses grow and create jobs. The VCEDA Seed Capital Matching Fund has assisted more than 200 new business startups since its establishment in 2017, with a return on investment ratio of 34:1 according to a 2021 Virginia Tech study . By promoting the success of these businesses, Lebanon can attract additional entrepreneurs and investors to the region.

## Section 11: Workforce Housing and Community Development

### Step 62: Launch the Modular Housing Production Facility

Lebanon must launch the modular housing production facility in the former Buster Brown Building in the Russell County Industrial Park, which received a $3 million loan from VCEDA . The facility, operated by Appalachian Highlands Housing Partners (AHHP), will manufacture modular housing units while providing workforce training in modern construction techniques . The facility will create 89 jobs within five years and manufacture at least 260 modular housing units in the first year, with the capacity to generate 300-400 additional housing units annually as the workforce is trained and grows . The facility will focus on providing housing for income earners between 80-120% of the Area Median Income (AMI), addressing the workforce housing shortage in the region . The initiative not only delivers high-quality, cost-effective homes for the community but also equips local workers with in-demand skills in modern construction techniques, creating lasting economic and social impact .

### Step 63: Implement the Small Developer Workshop Program

Lebanon must implement the Small Developer Workshop program, which received a $29,500 grant from VCEDA to assist with regional capacity-building training in workforce housing . The program provides training for public officials, public employees, and stakeholders on workforce housing development, addressing the shortage of affordable housing that constrains economic growth. The workshop covers topics including financing, site selection, construction techniques, and regulatory compliance, enabling participants to develop housing projects that meet the needs of the region's workforce. By participating in this program, Lebanon can build the capacity to develop additional housing projects, supporting population growth and economic expansion.

### Step 64: Utilize Modular Housing for Workforce Attraction

Lebanon must use the availability of modular housing to attract workers to the region, addressing the workforce shortage that constrains economic growth. The AHHP facility will produce housing units that can be marketed throughout the VCEDA region, providing affordable options for workers in manufacturing, healthcare, and other sectors . The availability of workforce housing is essential for attracting and retaining workers, particularly in a region where housing shortages have constrained employment growth. By promoting the availability of workforce housing to potential employers, Lebanon can create a virtuous cycle where new businesses attract workers, and the availability of housing supports population growth and further economic development.

## Section 12: Manufacturing Support and Expansion

### Step 65: Complete the Simmons Equipment Expansion

Lebanon must support the completion of the Simmons Equipment expansion, which received a $500,000 loan from VCEDA to finance building upfits and site improvements for the IDA's Russell Place property . The expansion of this mining equipment manufacturer from Tazewell County into Russell County represents a significant investment in the region and demonstrates the community's attractiveness to industrial employers. By completing this expansion and supporting Simmons Equipment's continued growth, Lebanon can retain jobs and attract additional manufacturing investment. The success of Simmons Equipment in Russell County provides a model for other industrial employers considering locating or expanding in the region.

### Step 66: Support the Tazewell County Meat Processing Facility

Lebanon must support the development of the Tazewell County meat processing facility, which received a loan closing deadline extension to June 11, 2027 . The facility, supported by VCEDA funding, will provide meat processing capacity for the region, enabling producers to capture greater value from their cattle production. The project was approved for an up to $800,000 loan to the Tazewell County IDA on December 14, 2023 . By supporting this facility and potentially developing a similar facility in Russell County, Lebanon can create value-added processing capacity that supports the region's cattle industry and creates jobs in food processing.

### Step 67: Pursue Additional Manufacturing and Distribution Projects

Lebanon must actively pursue additional manufacturing and distribution projects, building on the momentum of recent expansions. VCEDA has noted 24 active projects being worked, with manufacturing, distribution and logistics, information technology and seed capital all being active sectors . The updated industrial sites study currently being conducted by the Timmons Group will identify sites throughout the region suitable for future development for business and industry . By proactively marketing Lebanon's industrial sites and working with VCEDA to facilitate financing, the town can attract additional manufacturing and distribution operations that create jobs and economic activity.

## Section 13: Tourism Infrastructure and Outdoor Recreation

### Step 68: Complete Hillside Trail Park Development

Lebanon must complete the development of Hillside Trail Park, which received a $50,000 grant from the VCEDA Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Fund . The 11-acre site described by the Russell County IDA as the "gateway for broader tourism efforts tied to the Clinch River" will emphasize low-impact development and provide opportunities for environmental education and community engagement . Goals for the park include preserving and enhancing existing natural areas, improving positive environmental functions and water quality, providing accessible opportunities for environmental education, ensuring ADA-compliant access, increasing native habitat and biodiversity, and offering passive recreation options in a natural setting . The site lies within the Clinch River Watershed with water flowing through Big Cedar Creek into the Clinch River State Park, Virginia's first blueway state park . Work on the project has already started and will be completed in phases, with project improvements estimated to be completed within the next 18 months .

### Step 69: Develop the Intersection Business Park Opportunities

Lebanon must explore opportunities associated with the Intersection Business Park in Norton, which is developing new business leads through an initiative conducted in coordination with the Lonesome Pine Regional Industrial Facilities Authority . The business park represents a major regional industrial development opportunity, and new initiatives to generate leads for new businesses could create opportunities for Lebanon businesses to participate in the supply chain. By staying informed about developments at the Intersection Business Park and seeking opportunities for Russell County businesses to participate, Lebanon can benefit from regional industrial development.

### Step 70: Support River Hill Farms and Fly Fishing Tourism

Lebanon must support the expansion of River Hill Farms, a $10,000 VCEDA Seed Capital Grant recipient that is developing a premier fly fishing destination along Big Cedar Creek . The property, which fronts a one-mile stretch of pristine waters teeming with wild rainbow and brown trout, is developing a fishing lodge and guide service . The lodge, which is 75-80% complete, will be available for rental to those seeking a mountain getaway, with fly fishing packages available as an add-on . Future plans include adding yurts and cottages in a phased approach, creating a comprehensive outdoor tourism destination . By supporting the success of River Hill Farms, Lebanon can demonstrate the potential for nature-based tourism to create jobs and economic activity while preserving the region's natural resources.
