## Part One: Financial Institutions and Banks

### 1.1 First Bank & Trust Company

First Bank & Trust Company stands as the premier financial institution rooted in Lebanon, Virginia, with a remarkable history that began in a small basement where a group of Russell County farmers came together with a shared vision to create a bank that understood the land, valued hard work, and put people first . With 183 local shareholders investing $20 per share, the bank was built on a promise to serve neighbors and strengthen communities, and today it manages $4.1 billion in assets as of 2025 . The bank operates 34 offices across Virginia, Tennessee, and North Carolina, offering a full range of financial services including agricultural lending, business and commercial lending, trust and wealth management, and mortgage services . It is ranked as the 3rd largest agricultural bank in the United States and the 17th largest agricultural lender among all U.S. commercial banks by dollar volume, reflecting its deep-rooted commitment to supporting farmers, agribusinesses, and rural communities . First Bank & Trust has never merged with another bank and has maintained its independent roots, with its only acquisition being The First Commonwealth Bank in 2003, which expanded its reach to Wise and Norton, Virginia . The bank holds a Five-Star Superior Rating by BauerFinancial, the nation's leading independent bank rating firm, and is ranked 5th among the Top 10 Performing Banks in Virginia .

### 1.2 Virginia Coalfield Economic Development Authority (VCEDA)

The Virginia Coalfield Economic Development Authority serves as the primary engine of economic development finance for Lebanon and the broader region, created by the Virginia General Assembly in 1988 to enhance and diversify the region's economy and help create jobs . VCEDA manages funds for economic development projects from a percentage of the coal and natural gas severance taxes paid by coal and natural gas companies that operate in the region . The authority markets Southwest Virginia's e-Region and its focus on electronic information technology, energy, education, emerging technologies, and entrepreneurship, serving a seven-county region including Buchanan, Dickenson, Lee, Russell, Scott, Tazewell, and Wise counties and the City of Norton . VCEDA operates multiple financing programs, including the Coalfield Revolving Loan Fund, the Communities for Opportunity (CFO) Loan Fund, the Seed Capital Matching Fund, the Workforce Development and Training Fund, and the Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Fund .

## Part Two: Manufacturing and Industrial Businesses in Lebanon

### 2.1 Tate (Kingspan Company)

Tate is a global leader in mission-critical data center infrastructure solutions and a subsidiary of Kingspan Group PLC, with a history spanning more than 120 years of innovation and engineering leadership . The company has evolved from a raised access floor manufacturer to a comprehensive provider of advanced data center systems, including modular aisle containment, raised access floors, structural ceilings, airflow management solutions, and liquid-cooling technologies . Tate recently announced the expansion of its U.S. manufacturing operations with the acquisition of a 131,000-square-foot facility in Lebanon, Virginia, located at the former Alcoa manufacturing building at 705 Regional Park Road . The expansion, supported by an up to $4 million loan from VCEDA to the Russell County Industrial Development Authority, is expected to create up to 35 new full-time jobs within three years . The facility features open manufacturing area, office space, 13 docks and overhead doors, and is situated on an approximately 11-acre site adjacent to four-lane U.S. Route 19 . Headquartered in Columbia, Maryland, Tate supports hyperscale, colocation, and enterprise environments with fully integrated engineering, manufacturing, and innovation capabilities . The company's portfolio delivers floor-to-ceiling performance, enabling customers to build efficient, scalable, and sustainable data centers . Tate also operates a facility in St. Paul, Virginia, which received $10 million in New Markets Tax Credit financing from Rural Development Partners, creating over 170 quality FTE jobs .

### 2.3 Appalachian Highlands Housing Partners (AHHP)

Appalachian Highlands Housing Partners is a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation formed in 2024 by the Wellspring Foundation of Southwest Virginia to address workforce housing needs in the region . The organization is developing a modular housing production facility in the approximately 92,000-square-foot former Buster Brown Building in the Russell County Industrial Park, with support from an up to $3 million loan from VCEDA . The facility will manufacture at least 260 units of modular housing in the first year, with the capacity to generate 300-400 additional housing units annually as the workforce is trained and grows . The project anticipates the creation of 89 jobs within five years and also includes a workforce development component that focuses on construction training and education . The homes produced at the Lebanon facility will be available for purchase in any county in the VCEDA region, addressing the workforce housing shortage that has constrained regional growth .

### 2.4 Bates Family Farm, LLC

Bates Family Farm is a veteran-owned and operated agricultural business that raises goats and manufactures goat milk lotions and soaps, with its products sold in more than 1,000 retail and specialty stores across the United States . The company, established in Lee County in 2013 by Shannon and Joe Bates, relocated to Russell County in 2019 and has grown its herd from approximately 30 goats to about 200 goats . In 2023, the company invested roughly $1 million to relocate its manufacturing facility to the 40,000-square-foot former Acme grocery store building in Lebanon, a project expected to create 12 jobs . The company has received nearly $1 million in loans from VCEDA to the Russell County Industrial Development Authority for the purchase and renovation of the building, and Governor Youngkin approved a $70,000 grant from the AFID Fund, which Russell County matched locally . The company expects to increase production to $2 million worth of agricultural product (the value of the milk, not the finished products) over the next three years .

## Part Three: Economic Development Agencies and Government Partners

### 3.1 Russell County Industrial Development Authority (IDA)

The Russell County Industrial Development Authority serves as the primary local economic development organization for Lebanon and Russell County, facilitating business expansion and attraction through property acquisition, building renovations, and financing partnerships . The IDA has been instrumental in securing VCEDA funding for major projects including the modular housing manufacturing facility ($3 million), the Tate expansion ($4 million), the Bates Family Farm Acme Building renovations (nearly $1 million), and the Simmons Equipment expansion ($500,000) .

**Key Personnel:**
- **Ernie McFaddin:** Executive Director
- **Ron Blankenship:** Chairman, Russell County IDA Board

**Contact Information:**
- **Address:** Lebanon, VA
- **Phone:** (276) 889-0381

### 3.2 Virginia Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (VDACS)

VDACS works with local governments and industrial development authorities to secure agricultural projects and provides AFID grants for qualifying businesses . The agency worked with Russell County and the Russell County IDA to secure the Bates Family Farm project, with Governor Youngkin approving a $70,000 grant from the AFID Fund .

### 3.3 Virginia Tourism Corporation (VTC)

The Virginia Tourism Corporation operates the Special Events and Festivals Sponsorship Program, which provides matching grant funds for events and festivals that stimulate overnight visitation . VTC supports Lebanon's cultural tourism development through programs such as Cedar Fest funding and the promotion of the e-Region.

### 3.4 Appalachia Regional Commission (ARC)

The Appalachian Regional Commission provides grant funding and technical assistance to address economic and labor dislocations in coal communities through programs such as the POWER Initiative .

### 3.5 LENOWISCO Planning District Commission

LENOWISCO serves as a regional planning and development organization for the Virginia coalfield region, with Duane A. Miller serving as Executive Director and representing the agency on the VCEDA Board .

### 4.1 River Hill Farms (Harwood Cavalier Properties LLC)

River Hill Farms is developing a premier fly fishing destination along Big Cedar Creek in Lebanon, Virginia, on 65 acres which fronts a one-mile stretch of pristine waters teeming with wild rainbow and brown trout . The business recently received a $10,000 VCEDA Seed Capital Matching Grant, with funds being used for trout stocking, marketing, site development, and early construction of the River Hill Farms lodge . The business projects one full-time employee and three part-time employees within five years . The lodge, which is about 75-80% complete, will be available for rental to those seeking a mountain getaway, with fly fishing packages available as an add-on . Future plans include adding yurts and cottages in a phased approach, creating a comprehensive outdoor tourism destination .

## Part Five: Educational and Training Institutions

### 5.1 Southwest Virginia Community College (SWCC)

Southwest Virginia Community College is located in Richlands, approximately 20 miles from Lebanon, and serves as a key workforce development partner for the region . The college provides associate degrees, transfer programs, and workforce certifications in nursing, business, information technology, engineering technology, and skilled trades . The college has a total enrollment of 3,855 students, with 1,648 full-time and 2,207 part-time students, and a student/faculty ratio of 15:1 . The Small Business Development Center (SBDC) at SWCC provides guidance on VCEDA Seed Capital Grant applications and other business development services . The college recently received a $999,868 USDA grant for distance learning equipment and a $250,000 grant from VCEDA for workforce development and training .
