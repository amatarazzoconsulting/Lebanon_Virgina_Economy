# Part Two: The Championship Golf Course

## Chapter 3: Design Principles for a World-Class Golf Course

The championship golf course envisioned for Lebanon must embody the highest standards of golf course architecture while respecting and enhancing the region's natural beauty and ecological sensitivity. The design principles that guide the course development prioritize four elements: integration with the existing topography, strategic playability for golfers of all skill levels, environmental sustainability, and aesthetic excellence. The course is intended to be a destination that attracts golfers from across the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast regions, complementing the existing cultural tourism assets and creating a comprehensive visitor experience that encourages extended stays.

The course layout should embrace the natural topography of the Clinch Mountain region, utilizing the rolling hills, ridges, and valleys to create a routing that is both challenging and visually stunning. The Golf Course Builders Association of America certification study emphasizes that golf course design includes considerably more than just hole routing; it includes contour layout, water feature design, clearing lines, green and bunker layout, bridges and paths, irrigation, seeding and planting, specifications for materials, and overall land planning . Careful considerations are always made for utilization of the existing topography, and the best courses are those that appear to have emerged naturally from the landscape . For the Lebanon course, this means routing holes along the natural contours of the land, using ridges as strategic features, and positioning greens and tees to take advantage of the mountain views that characterize the region.

The site selection for the course must consider the size requirements for a championship facility. Studies of golf course development have identified that successful golf courses typically require parcels of 150 or more acres, with many courses occupying 200 to 300 acres for 18 holes and clubhouse facilities . For the Lebanon championship course, a minimum of 200 acres is recommended, with the ability to expand to 300 acres if additional amenities such as a driving range, practice facilities, and short game area are included. The site should be in all or partly wooded areas, allowing the preservation of significant tree cover that contributes to the character of the course. The site should also have adequate water resources for irrigation, with access to surface water or groundwater that can support the course's water needs without compromising local water supplies. The Clinch River watershed, with its abundant water resources, provides a natural advantage for golf course development in the Lebanon area.

The hole routing should provide variety and strategic interest, with a mix of holes that challenge different aspects of the golfer's game. A championship course typically includes par 3, par 4, and par 5 holes, with the par 5s providing opportunities for risk-reward decisions and the par 3s requiring precision shot-making. The routing should include holes that play in different directions to account for wind conditions, and the greens should be positioned to provide strategic angles of approach that reward thoughtful play. The design should incorporate the natural features of the site, including existing water features, rock outcroppings, and changes in elevation, to create a course that is both challenging and memorable. The use of the Clinch Mountain range as a backdrop for key holes would create spectacular views that enhance the golfing experience.

The greens construction must meet the highest standards to provide consistent, high-quality playing surfaces. The USGA green construction specification, which is the industry standard for championship courses, includes laying a 4-inch layer of gravel on the subgrade, then adding a 2-inch layer of choker sand, and following up with a 12-inch layer of compacted root zone mix . Each layer must follow the contour of the finish green surface, and the 2-inch layer of choker sand is specified to allow better rooting and prevent the root zone mix from entering the pea gravel drains under the green . The root zone mix typically has a sand to peat moss ratio of 80/20 by volume, and the desired range of medium and coarse sand is 65 to 80 percent . Permeability for bentgrass greens should be from 14 to 17 inches per hour, and many green specifications require that an impervious plastic barrier be placed around the green perimeter to prevent capillary movement between the greens mix and surrounding soils . The use of the California green construction method, which uses straight sand rather than a root zone mix, is also an option for certain design approaches.

The bunkers should be constructed with a variety of shapes and contours that provide visual interest and strategic challenge. The recommended slope ratios for bunkers can vary, with steeper grades requiring extensive drainage and the banks requiring sod cover as opposed to seed to prevent erosion . Trap sand is purchased by the ton rather than the cubic yard, with the weight varying with moisture content, and is typically installed at a depth of 6 to 8 inches . The sand should be a high-quality bunker sand that meets the specifications for drainage and playability, with consistent particle size and shape that prevents compaction and provides predictable playing characteristics. The color of the sand should complement the surrounding landscape, with bunkers integrated into the design to appear as natural features of the terrain.

The fairways should be constructed to provide both playability and strategic interest, with widths that challenge golfers of different skill levels and landing zones that reward accuracy. The finish grading for fairways requires skilled shaping to achieve the contour desired by the architect, with slopes that should be no more than 4:1 to facilitate ease of maintenance . A minimum slope of 2 percent is required to provide adequate surface drainage, and the fairways should be designed to allow water to drain away from play areas while minimizing erosion . The fairway construction should include provisions for irrigation, with sprinkler heads positioned to provide coverage for the entire playing surface while minimizing water waste. The fairway design should also consider the routing of cart paths, with concrete paths that require fiber mesh or rebar placed in concrete for strength .

## Chapter 4: Turfgrass Selection for Lebanon's Climate

The selection of turfgrass for Lebanon's championship golf course is a critical decision that will determine the quality of playing conditions, the sustainability of the course, and the cost of maintenance. Virginia is a transition zone state noted for its hot, humid summers and cold, wet winters, which presents significant challenges for turfgrass selection and management . As the Virginia Tech Turfgrass Team reminds the public, "In Virginia, we can grow eight different turfgrasses, not very well!" . However, with careful selection based on location, use, and management commitment, successful turfgrass establishment and maintenance is achievable . The mountain region of Virginia, where Lebanon is located, is likely best suited for cool-season turf, with USDA Horticultural zones 5b-6b providing conditions that support the growth of cool-season grasses . Zoysiagrass has satisfactory cold tolerance, making it suitable as a lawn grass in this region, though the price of seed or vegetative materials and the slow rate of establishment are typical limiting factors .

For the greens, the choice of turfgrass is particularly important as greens must provide the highest quality playing surfaces and withstand the highest levels of traffic. Bentgrass (Agrostis stolonifera) is the traditional choice for putting greens in cool-season climates, providing a dense, fine-textured surface that can be mown to very low heights. However, bentgrass is susceptible to heat stress and disease pressure during the hot, humid summers typical of Virginia's mountain region, requiring intensive management to maintain quality. Creeping bentgrass cultivars have been developed with improved heat tolerance and disease resistance, and these cultivars are the recommended choice for greens in the Lebanon region. For the greens in the championship course, a fescue/bent mixture such as Limagrain MM10, which was used for a new green construction at Leighton Buzzard Golf Club, provides the density and fine texture required for championship putting surfaces .

For the fairways and tees, tall fescue (Schedonorus arundinaceus) is the recommended choice for Lebanon's climate, providing excellent drought tolerance, deep root systems, and good wear tolerance . The turf-type varieties of tall fescue have fine to medium leaf texture and greatly enhanced density characteristics, and they are managed as a bunch-type grass that produces lots of tillers . The grass has the deepest root system of the cool-season lawn grasses grown in this area, which is an important strength for utilizing moisture deeper in the soil . Tall fescue has excellent drought avoidance characteristics, rapid germination rates of 4 to 7 days, early spring greening, moderate shade tolerance, and adaptation to a wide range of soils . The weaknesses of tall fescue include a high mowing requirement during active growing periods, limited to no recuperative potential, and susceptibility to disease, particularly Rhizoctonia blight (Brown Patch) . However, with proper management, tall fescue can provide high-quality playing surfaces throughout the growing season.

For the rough areas, mixtures of tall fescue and Kentucky bluegrass can provide the density, color, and wear tolerance needed for areas that receive less frequent mowing and less intensive management . Virginia's sod producers have long planted a sod crop that is 90% turf-type tall fescue and 10% Kentucky bluegrass by weight (resulting in an approximate 50/50 seed count), and this mixture has continued to do well as a perennial turf in appearance and performance across the state . Each of the grasses in the mixture provides its relative strengths for the course: spring greening and better drought tolerance for tall fescue, and dark color and recuperative potential for Kentucky bluegrass . The mixture provides a more predictable visual quality and performance than either grass alone, making it an excellent choice for rough areas where consistent appearance and playability are desired.

The integration of native grasses and naturalized areas is an important consideration for the Lebanon course, contributing to the aesthetic quality of the course and reducing maintenance requirements. Native grasses, which are adapted to local conditions and require less water and fertilizer than introduced turfgrasses, can be used in out-of-play areas to create a natural look that complements the surrounding landscape. The Hillside Trail Park project, with its emphasis on low-impact development, environmental education, and native habitat and biodiversity, demonstrates the community's commitment to environmental stewardship that can be reflected in the golf course design . The use of native grasses and naturalized areas also contributes to the course's sustainability by reducing water use and maintenance inputs while providing habitat for native wildlife.

The selection of turfgrasses for the Lebanon championship course should also consider the planned maintenance program, as different grasses have different requirements for mowing height, fertility, irrigation, and pest management. The Virginia Tech Turfgrass Team emphasizes that "a quality lawn results from using the right grass species and/or variety, proper planting and establishment, and sound management" . For a championship course, this means developing a comprehensive maintenance plan that includes regular mowing at the appropriate heights, balanced fertilization, pest management, irrigation, and cultural practices such as aeration and topdressing. The maintenance plan should be developed in consultation with turfgrass experts from Virginia Tech or other research institutions to ensure that best management practices are followed.

## Chapter 5: Materials and Equipment for Golf Course Construction

The construction of a championship golf course requires substantial quantities of materials and specialized equipment to achieve the precise grading, drainage, irrigation, and planting specifications required for high-quality playing surfaces. The Golf Course Builders Association of America certification study provides detailed specifications for the materials and equipment used in golf course construction, including earthmoving, drainage, irrigation, and planting . For the Lebanon course, the material quantities and equipment requirements must be calculated based on the course design, the site conditions, and the construction schedule. The use of local materials where possible can reduce costs and support the local economy, but the quality of materials must meet the specifications required for championship course construction.

Earthmoving is typically the largest single component of golf course construction, requiring heavy equipment to move large quantities of soil and shape the land to the architect's specifications. On most projects, the most efficient way to move soil is with a wheel scraper, and a 12-15 cubic yard scraper can typically move 800 to 1,000 cubic yards per day of dry structural clay over distances of 500 to 1,000 feet . Many projects utilize off-road trucks for earthmoving, a method referred to as "top loading," and the main factors for calculating earthmoving production are soil conditions, distance, and cycle times . Soil volume is measured in three categories: bank (in its natural state), loose (after excavation), and compacted (after placement and compaction) . Careful consideration must be made with regard to over-compaction of the soil, and low ground pressure (LGP) tracked equipment is used to minimize compaction . For the Lebanon course, earthmoving equipment should include wheel scrapers, dozers, excavators, graders, and compactors, with the specific equipment selection based on the site conditions and the construction schedule.

The drainage system is essential for the health of the turf and the playability of the course, requiring the installation of perforated drainage pipes, gravel, and sand to remove excess water from the soil profile. The drainage system for greens and tees requires V-shaped ditches cut into the formation, with perforated drainage pipe installed and topped up with washed drainage gravel . A gravel carpet is then spread across the area, followed by a blinding layer of fine gravel and finally a rootzone layer to create a surface ready for final grading and planting . For the Lebanon course, the drainage system should be designed to handle the 45 inches of annual rainfall that the region receives, with adequate capacity to remove excess water quickly and prevent waterlogging of the playing surfaces. The drainage materials required include perforated pipe (typically 4-inch diameter), gravel (various grades from 1/4 inch to 1 inch), and sand (for the rootzone mix and choker layer). Thrust blocking with concrete is required at all changes in direction of the pipe path for bell ring tite pipe connections .

The irrigation system is essential for maintaining the health and quality of the turf, particularly during the dry periods that can occur in the Virginia summer. The irrigation system for a championship golf course includes a pumping system, distribution pipes, sprinkler heads, and a computerized control system . The pumping systems use either turbine or centrifugal pumps, with groups of pumps placed in parallel to increase flow or in series to increase pressure . The piping system is most commonly piped with PVC pipe, though other material such as polyethylene is also used, and the system is zoned by gate or ball valves that have the lowest friction losses per like sizes of all valves made . The irrigation control systems and their communication networks between centrals and field units are typically hydraulic, electric, phone modem, or radio wave-based . For the Lebanon course, a computerized irrigation system with weather sensors and soil moisture monitors would provide the precision needed to maintain the quality of the playing surfaces while conserving water. The system should be designed to provide coverage for all playing surfaces, with sprinkler heads selected for the specific application based on the size and shape of the areas being irrigated.

The planting materials required for the course include sod for the greens, tees, and fairways, and seed for the rough areas and naturalized zones. For the greens, sod is the preferred establishment method as it provides an instant playing surface and reduces the time required for establishment. The turf for the 15th green at Leighton Buzzard Golf Club was installed using USGA specification greens turf, and the same approach would be appropriate for the Lebanon course . For the tees and fairways, sod is also recommended for rapid establishment, with a Ryegrass, Fescue, and Smooth Stalk mixture providing the density and wear tolerance required . For the rough areas and naturalized zones, seed is the preferred establishment method, with appropriate grass mixtures selected for the specific conditions. The seed for the surrounds at Leighton Buzzard was Limagrain Turfmaster, a Ryegrass, Fescue, and Smooth Stalk mixture, and the same mixture would be suitable for the Lebanon course . For the greens, the 14th Green was seeded with Limagrain MM10, a fescue/bent mixture, which would provide the fine texture and density required for championship putting surfaces .

The construction of clubhouse and support facilities requires additional materials and equipment, including concrete, steel, wood, and finishing materials. The clubhouse should be designed to complement the architectural character of the region while providing the amenities expected by golfers, including locker rooms, dining facilities, pro shop, and event space. The cart paths should be constructed to provide access to all holes while minimizing the impact on the playing surfaces, with common path materials including concrete, asphalt, and crushed rock . The paths should be designed to meet ADA accessibility requirements, with appropriate width, grade, and surface texture. The landscaping for the clubhouse and surrounding areas should use native plants that complement the natural beauty of the region and require minimal maintenance once established. The Hillside Trail Park project, with its emphasis on preserving and enhancing existing natural areas, increasing native habitat and biodiversity, and ensuring ADA-compliant access, provides a model for integrating the course facilities with the surrounding landscape .

# Part Three: The Arts and Entertainment Ecosystem

## Chapter 7: The Crooked Road and Lebanon's Musical Heritage

The Crooked Road: Virginia's Heritage Music Trail is the foundation of Lebanon's arts and entertainment ecosystem, providing a recognized brand and established network of venues and events that can be leveraged for economic development. The 333-mile driving trail links over 60 music venues across Southwest Virginia and was designated as Virginia's Heritage Music Trail by the Virginia Assembly in 2004 . The Crooked Road was created not just to showcase the arts, but also to fill in some of the gaps and to help communities overcome some of the financial hurdles that they faced as the region's economy changed . The trail supports economic development by celebrating, promoting, and preserving the region's unique musical and cultural heritage, and the Virginia General Assembly commended the trail in 2024 for leading to increased visitor spending, tourism-related taxes, and job creation in the region .

The Crooked Road's affiliated venues and festivals program brings special attention to the region's most exemplary venues, jams, and festivals . The program includes three classifications: Crooked Road Jams (weekly, monthly, or yearly events highlighting traditional music in a jam setting), Crooked Road Festivals (annual festivals presenting primarily traditional music), and Crooked Road Affiliates (venues within the Crooked Road territory presenting traditional music on a regular basis) . Lebanon's Cedar Fest has been recognized as an outstanding festival, winning the Southwest Virginia Tourism award for Outstanding Festival of the Year in the under 10,000 attendees category, and the festival continues to grow in popularity, bringing visitors from all over the region and outside the region . The affiliation with The Crooked Road provides the festival with promotional support, including listing on the Crooked Road website, promotion through Crooked Road media, and the opportunity to display the Affiliate banner at the event .

The economic impact of The Crooked Road has been recognized by the Virginia General Assembly, with HJR216 commending the trail in recognition of its 20th anniversary and noting that the trail has reportedly led to increased visitor spending, tourism-related taxes, and job creation in the area . The Tobacco Region Revitalization Commission has long supported The Crooked Road with over $4 million in grants over the years, funding everything from community signage to economic impact studies . The most recent support includes $50,000 towards revitalizing the Rex Theatre in Galax, VA, one of the major venues on the Crooked Road . For Lebanon, the affiliation with The Crooked Road and the recognition of Cedar Fest provide a foundation for expanding the arts and entertainment offerings, with the potential to attract additional grants and funding for venue development and programming.

The opportunity for Lebanon to develop new arts and entertainment venues is supported by the existing network of The Crooked Road's affiliated venues and the region's commitment to cultural development. The Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation's 2024 Annual Report highlights the success of the third annual Southwest Virginia Tourism Awards, new initiatives like the Artisan Conference, and the Stories from Southwest Virginia podcast, all of which demonstrate the region's commitment to leveraging its cultural assets . The development of a performing arts center in Lebanon, similar to the Jettie Baker Center in Clintwood, could provide a venue for music performances, theater, and community events. ## Chapter 8: Venues, Events, and Programming for Arts and Entertainment

The development of arts and entertainment venues in Lebanon should build on the existing foundation of The Crooked Road and the Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation, creating a range of offerings that appeal to visitors and residents alike. The primary venue should be a performing arts center that can host music performances, theater productions, community events, and conferences. The center should be designed to meet the needs of both touring artists and local productions, with flexible seating that can accommodate audiences of different sizes. The center should include a main stage with professional sound and lighting systems, a lobby for receptions and gatherings, and backstage facilities for artists. The design should reflect the architectural character of the region, with materials and finishes that complement the surrounding landscape. The Jettie Baker Center in Clintwood, which received VCEDA funding for HVAC improvements, provides an example of the type of venue that can be developed and maintained through public-private partnerships .

The performing arts center should be complemented by outdoor venues that take advantage of the region's natural beauty and pleasant summer weather. The Hillside Trail Park, with its emphasis on low-impact development and environmental education, provides a model for outdoor venues that integrate with the natural landscape . An outdoor amphitheater, similar to the venue that hosts Cedar Fest, could provide a setting for outdoor concerts during the summer months, with the Clinch Mountain range providing a stunning backdrop . The amphitheater should be designed with appropriate sound, lighting, and seating to provide a high-quality experience for both performers and audiences. The venue should be accessible to visitors with disabilities and should include adequate parking and support facilities. The integration of the amphitheater with the Hillside Trail Park could provide opportunities for visitors to explore the natural surroundings before or after events.

The programming for the arts and entertainment venues should be diverse and appealing to visitors of different interests and ages. The programming should include performances of traditional music, including old-time, bluegrass, gospel, and early country music, which are the focus of The Crooked Road's mission . The programming should also include contemporary music, theater, dance, and other performing arts that appeal to a broader audience. The programming should be scheduled throughout the year to attract visitors during all seasons, with special events and festivals that create peak periods of visitation. The Cedar Fest, with its two-day schedule and growing popularity, provides a model for a signature event that attracts visitors from beyond the region . The development of additional festivals, such as a bluegrass festival, an artisan festival, or a food and wine festival, could expand the calendar of events and attract different audiences.

The arts and entertainment offerings should also include visual arts and crafts, building on the foundation of the 'Round the Mountain Artisan Network. The Southwest Virginia Cultural Center & Marketplace serves as a hub for the artisan network, showcasing the products of the region's craftspeople and providing a retail experience that appeals to visitors . The development of galleries and studios in downtown Lebanon could provide additional venues for visual arts and crafts, supporting a vibrant arts community that attracts visitors and enhances the quality of life for residents. The Artisan Conference, a new initiative highlighted in the Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation's 2024 Annual Report, demonstrates the region's commitment to supporting the artisan community and could provide opportunities for collaboration with the Lebanon arts community . The Stories from Southwest Virginia podcast, another new initiative, provides a platform for sharing the stories of the region's artists and craftspeople, building awareness and interest in the region's cultural assets .

The arts and entertainment venues should also serve as community gathering spaces, hosting events that bring residents together and strengthen community bonds. The venues should be available for rental by community groups for events such as weddings, receptions, and meetings. The venues should be integrated into the fabric of downtown Lebanon, with connections to restaurants, shops, and other businesses. The development of the venues should be coordinated with the town's streetscape improvements and sidewalk projects, creating a walkable, attractive environment that appeals to visitors and residents alike. The recent investments in the Community Center, pool, Russell Theater, and pavilion demonstrate the commitment to developing community gathering spaces that enhance the quality of life and attract visitors . The Russell Theater, which has been recently completed, could serve as an additional venue for arts and entertainment programming, providing a historic setting for performances and events.

The marketing of the arts and entertainment offerings should be coordinated with the marketing of the golf course, creating a comprehensive visitor experience that is promoted to potential visitors in the region's primary and secondary markets. The marketing should highlight the unique combination of golf and culture that Lebanon offers, emphasizing the opportunity to play a championship golf course and experience the region's musical and cultural heritage. The marketing should target different demographic groups, with different messages for families, couples, and groups. The marketing should use a variety of channels, including digital marketing, print advertising, public relations, and partnerships with travel organizations. The Virginia Tourism Corporation's hub-and-spoke tourism model, which creates multi-day itineraries by using a special event or festival as the main lure and encouraging visitors to extend their stays to experience local attractions, provides a framework for marketing the integrated visitor experience . The success of Cedar Fest in attracting visitors and winning awards demonstrates the effectiveness of marketing the region's unique cultural offerings .

## Chapter 9: The Economic Impact of Golf, Arts, and Entertainment

The combined economic impact of golf, arts, and entertainment development in Lebanon is substantial, with the potential to create jobs, generate tax revenue, and support the growth of local businesses. Based on the economic impact study of Virginia's golf economy, a championship golf course in Lebanon could be expected to generate direct, indirect, and induced economic impacts that support jobs, tax revenue, and business activity . The Virginia golf economy in 2012 contributed $2.513 billion in total economic output and supported 29,919 jobs, with golf facility operations alone generating $714.4 million in direct economic impact and supporting 18,550 jobs . The hospitality and tourism sector related to golf generated $422.8 million in direct impact, supporting 9,245 jobs . While these figures represent the entire state, they demonstrate the substantial economic potential of golf development. For Lebanon, a single championship course, integrated with the region's existing cultural tourism assets, could be expected to generate significant economic benefits.

The economic impact of the arts and entertainment sector is also substantial, with The Crooked Road's economic impact recognized by the Virginia General Assembly for leading to increased visitor spending, tourism-related taxes, and job creation in the region . The Tobacco Region Revitalization Commission has provided over $4 million in grants to The Crooked Road, funding signage, economic impact studies, and venue improvements, demonstrating the economic potential of cultural tourism . The Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation's 2024 Annual Report highlights the transformative investments in outdoor recreation and downtown revitalization that have supported the creative economy . The opening of two new destination centers, the completion of a riverwalk, and the construction of a new competition-grade bike and skate park all contribute to the region's tourism infrastructure and economic development . The Cedar Fest's success in attracting visitors and winning the Southwest Virginia Tourism award for Outstanding Festival of the Year demonstrates the economic potential of arts and entertainment events .

The integration of golf with arts and entertainment creates synergistic economic benefits that exceed the sum of the individual sectors. The concept of cultural tourism, where visitors are drawn to a region by its cultural assets and then engage with other recreational activities, suggests that a championship golf course located near The Crooked Road's music venues and the Southwest Virginia Cultural Center & Marketplace would create a powerful draw for extended-stay visitors. The Virginia Tourism Corporation's hub-and-spoke tourism model, which creates multi-day itineraries by using a special event or festival as the main lure and encouraging visitors to extend their stays to experience local attractions, provides a framework for integrating golf into the region's tourism ecosystem . Visitors who come for golf may stay for music; those who come for a festival may extend their stay to play the course. This extended stay translates into increased spending on accommodations, dining, shopping, and other services, supporting local businesses and creating jobs.

The creation of jobs in the golf, arts, and entertainment sectors is substantial, with both direct employment in the sectors and indirect employment in supporting industries. The golf sector creates direct employment in course maintenance, clubhouse operations, and instruction, as well as indirect employment in equipment sales, hospitality, and tourism . The arts and entertainment sector creates direct employment in venues, festivals, and cultural organizations, as well as indirect employment in restaurants, hotels, and retail businesses. The workforce development initiatives already underway in the region, including the Hospitality and Tourism Management Trainee and Culinary, Restaurant, and Event Management Trainee Pilot Program at the University of Virginia's College at Wise, will help ensure that the workforce is available to fill these positions . The availability of well-paying jobs in these sectors will help attract and retain young people in the region, reversing the population decline that has affected many rural areas.

The tax revenue generated by golf, arts, and entertainment development is an important benefit for Lebanon and Russell County, providing funding for public services and infrastructure. The Virginia Beach study projected that five upscale public golf courses would generate about $1.8 million in annual sales and amusement taxes . The economic impact of The Crooked Road has been recognized by the Virginia General Assembly for leading to increased tourism-related taxes in the region . For Lebanon, the development of a championship golf course and arts and entertainment venues would generate sales tax revenue from visitor spending, lodging tax revenue from overnight stays, and property tax revenue from the development. This revenue would support funding for public services, including education, public safety, and infrastructure, benefiting both residents and visitors.

## Chapter 10: Funding and Partnerships for Golf, Arts, and Entertainment Development

The funding for golf, arts, and entertainment development in Lebanon is available through a combination of public and private sources, including VCEDA loans and grants, the Tobacco Region Revitalization Commission, and private investment. The VCEDA Tourism Capital Improvement Matching Fund provides loans and grants to assist with the development of tourism-related capital improvements that are part of larger regional tourism initiatives enhancing the region's economy . The fund has supported projects including the $50,000 grant for HVAC improvements at the Jettie Baker Center in Dickenson County and the $50,000 grant for the Hillside Trail Park in Lebanon . The VCEDA Coalfield Workforce Development and Training Fund provides loans and/or grants for workforce development and training, including curriculum development, training equipment, and training facilities construction . The $230,016 grant to the University of Virginia's College at Wise for the Hospitality and Tourism Management Trainee and Culinary, Restaurant, and Event Management Trainee Pilot Program demonstrates the availability of funding for workforce development .

The Tobacco Region Revitalization Commission has provided over $4 million in grants to The Crooked Road and continues to fund cultural and economic development projects in the region . The most recent support includes $50,000 towards revitalizing the Rex Theatre in Galax, VA . The Virginia Tourism Corporation's Special Events and Festivals Sponsorship Program provides matching grant funds for events and festivals that stimulate overnight visitation, with over $885,733 awarded to 88 special events and festivals in the most recent funding round . The program requires a match from local partners, with Lebanon providing $5,000 to match the $5,000 grant for Cedar Fest . The Virginia Tourism Corporation's hub-and-spoke tourism model provides a framework for developing multi-day itineraries that encourage visitors to extend their stays and increase spending . These funding sources provide the resources necessary to support the development of golf, arts, and entertainment venues and programming in Lebanon.

The private investment for golf, arts, and entertainment development is available from a variety of sources, including developers, investors, and business owners. A study of potential golf development in Virginia Beach found that the cost of building new courses would range from $6.5 to $10.2 million each, including clubhouses and other amenities, excluding land costs . For the Lebanon championship course, a similar investment would be required, with funding available from private developers and investors who see the potential for a profitable golf operation. Private investment would also be needed for the development of accommodations, restaurants, and other tourism-related businesses that support the golf and entertainment economy. The availability of public funding from VCEDA and the Tobacco Commission can leverage private investment, reducing the risk for private investors and increasing the probability of project success.

The partnerships for golf, arts, and entertainment development should include local government, economic development organizations, educational institutions, and private businesses. The Russell County Industrial Development Authority, which has been active in developing the Hillside Trail Park and supporting the expansion of Bates Family Farm, provides a model for public-private partnerships that support economic development . The Town of Lebanon, which has invested in streetscape improvements, new sidewalks, and community amenities, provides the infrastructure necessary to support tourism development . The Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation and The Crooked Road provide the cultural infrastructure and branding that are essential for attracting visitors . The educational institutions, including the University of Virginia's College at Wise and Southwest Virginia Community College, provide the workforce training that is essential for the success of the tourism industry . The private businesses, including hotels, restaurants, and shops, provide the services that visitors need and generate the economic activity that drives the tourism economy.

The implementation of the golf, arts, and entertainment development should be phased to manage risk and build momentum. The first phase should focus on the development of the championship golf course, which provides the anchor for the tourism economy. The second phase should focus on the development of arts and entertainment venues, building on the existing foundation of The Crooked Road and the Southwest Virginia Cultural Heritage Foundation. The third phase should focus on the development of accommodations and additional amenities that support the tourism industry. The phased approach allows for the testing of the market and the adaptation of the strategy based on experience, reducing the risk of over-investment and ensuring that the development is sustainable over the long term.
